# ‘More foodgrain lost due to pests, diseases’

  * Special Correspondent

Agricultural College and Research Institute in

Challenges now:Participants at a convention and symposium held at Agricultural
College and Research Institute in Madurai.

Though the country had become self-reliant in production of foodgrains and
cereals, 22 to 25 per cent of losses were caused by pests and diseases, said
P.K. Chakrabarty, Assistant Director General, Indian Council of Agricultural
Research, on Thursday.

Inaugurating a three-day annual convention and national symposium on
'Challenges and management approaches for crop diseases of national
importance,' organised to commemorate the golden jubilee celebrations of
Agricultural College and Research Institute here, Dr. Chakrabarty said
climatic change, change in cropping pattern, indiscriminate use of botanical
modules, mono cropping system, insufficient domestic quarantine and
introduction of newer pesticides in the market were the reasons for the
increased number of pathogens.

Efficient use of pesticides and introduction of uniform integrated pest and
disease management system for all crops, which would avoid confusion among
farmers, would play a key role in management of pests and diseases, he said.

K. Ramasamy, Vice-Chancellor, Tamil Nadu Agricultural University, in his
presidential address, insisted on development of suitable pest control
mechanisms that would help farmers in protecting their crops.

Those who addressed the inaugural session included A.N. Sabalpara and Pokkar
Rawal of Indian Society of Mycology and Plant Pathology; R. Ravindran,
Registrar, M. Maheswaran, K. Ramaraju and N. Ragupathi, Directors, TNAU, and
C. Chinnusamy, Dean, Agricultural College and Research Institute.
